Set a SMART goal

How it works?: Specific. Measurable. Attainable. Relevant. Time-Bound. read more: Read more

❓Is it for you❓

You probably heard about SMART goals many times in your life. But have you set one yourself?

Does the way you set goals helps you achieve them? If yes - then maybe you don’t need Aimily and you could share your success story and your personal know-how with us. But if you have tried many different things and still feel overwhelmed when you set a new goal, keep procrastinating and then become angry at yourself for not doing anything - then you are in the right place to be.

❓Is SMART goal the solution❓

Just setting a SMART goal doesn’t guarantee you 100% success, and 2020 showed us how things might change and that we can’t control everything. But this is not a reason to just go with a flow.

❓Why SMART goal❓

Your brain needs to know the direction you want to head.

The more SPECIFIC you are, the more details you include - the clearer guidelines your brain gets and starts looking for all the possibilities to make that thing you want to happen.

You need to be clear with yourself as well - how will you MEASURE the result, how will you know that your goal is achieved?

While we totally support the idea of dreaming big and believe that whatever you can imagine - the world has the capacity to deliver, to make things work for you faster and smoother we recommend setting an ATTAINABLE goal. Meaning that you already have some foundations, resources to start with.

RELEVANT. Ask yourself 5 WHYs: why do you want this? And whatever your first answer is - dig deeper - why do you want that? Repeat 5 times. Make sure this is something YOU want, not your friend, family, neighbour.

As we mentioned, once your brain knows what to look for, while you are sleeping, working, walking, talking, your brain looks for the opportunities to make this happen. So if your goal is to buy a house, your brain might take it as - in this lifetime you want to buy a house and will relax for the next 10-20 years. But is it what you want? If you have an idea - whether it is 1 year or 5 years, make sure you write it down and make your goal TIME-BOUND, that way your brain will start working straight away.
